About the Campaign

About Rewear

Rewear is a youth-led campus campaign focused on reducing fashion waste through reuse, swaps, and thrift culture. We are student-run, peer-powered, and built to last.

Mission

Empower students to make low-impact style choices while keeping fashion creative, accessible, and social. We believe sustainability is not a sacrifice — it's an upgrade.

How We Work

We run monthly swap drives, peer styling sessions, ambassador programs, and digital awareness campaigns centered on garment lifespan and circular fashion.

Campaign Design

Persuasion Strategy

The Rewear campaign is built on a multi-layered persuasion model. Each channel and message is deliberately matched to a communication strategy that targets a different driver of behaviour change.

💚

Pathos — Emotional Appeal

Posters and social content use vivid imagery and personal accountability to create an emotional connection between individual clothing choices and real-world environmental impact. Messages like "Your closet is full. The planet is running out of room." make abstract problems feel personal and urgent.

📊

Logos — Evidence & Logic

Data-driven messages target students' rational self-interest: financial savings ($1,200/year), carbon impact (10% of global emissions), and garment lifespan (worn only 7 times on average). Facts are translated into relatable comparisons — "equal to not driving for 3 days" — to maximize comprehension and recall.

🤝

Ethos — Credibility & Social Norms

Peer testimonials and descriptive social norm statements ("3 in 5 students already rewear outfits") reduce the perceived social risk of behaviour change. When students see their peers already participating, the barrier to joining drops significantly. Campus ambassador voices carry more trust than institutional messaging.

📣

Social Proof & Commitment

The public pledge mechanic leverages commitment-consistency: once someone signs, they are more likely to follow through. The visible pledge counter ("200+ pledgers") reinforces that this is a growing movement — not a fringe position — using social proof to accelerate adoption.
