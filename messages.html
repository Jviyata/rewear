Persuasion Strategy

Campaign Messages

Every message below is grounded in a named persuasion strategy — emotional appeal (Pathos), evidence-based logic (Logos), credibility and norms (Ethos), or social-norm nudges. Together they form a multi-channel campaign ready to deploy across print, digital, and in-person activations.

Persuasion Strategy Key

Pathos

Connects through emotion — guilt, pride, hope, or urgency — to motivate action.

Logos

Uses data and facts to make the logical case for change.

Ethos / Social Norms

Builds credibility and belonging by showing what peers and experts are doing.

✅ Personal Action Checklist

Small, concrete habits that reinforce the campaign's core message.

  1. Buy Second-Hand First

    Check thrift stores, vintage shops, or peer resale platforms before buying anything new. Save money and cut demand for new production.

  2. Repeat Outfits Confidently

    Every repeat wear is a win. Challenge the idea that every event needs a new look — and show others it's stylish to do so.

  3. Join or Host a Monthly Swap

    Bring clothes you no longer need and take home something "new-to-you" for free. One event can divert dozens of garments from landfill.

  4. Repair Instead of Replace

    Sew tears, replace buttons, or take items to a tailor. Extending garment life by 9 months reduces its carbon footprint by 20–30%.

  5. Unfollow Overconsumption Content

    Haul culture drives impulsive purchases. Curate your feed to reflect the values you want to live — and follow creators who do the same.

  6. Share the Numbers

    Fashion = 10% of global carbon emissions. Garments worn avg. 7 times. Tell one person today. Word-of-mouth is the original campaign.
